# Evaluating and Mitigating Socioeconomic Bias in Automatic Speech Recognition Systems

## Project Aim
Recent research in Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) has increasingly highlighted systematic
disparities across different speaker groups (i.e., gender, age, or accent). Moreover, emerging
evidence suggests that these disparities also extend to socioeconomic status (SES), revealing
a new and underexplored dimension of algorithmic bias. Speakers from lower socioeconomic
backgrounds often exhibit distinct linguistic, acoustic, and environmental characteristics, such
as regional dialects and varied pronunciation patterns, that are underrepresented in current ASR
datasets. Consequently, ASR models often yield higher word error rates for these groups due
to the social inequalities and limiting accessibility. As a result, a key open question remains:
how can we accurately measure the performance and bias of ASR systems across socioeconomic
differences? This study aims to investigate socioeconomic bias in ASR systems by evaluating
model performance across SES groups as well as analyzing linguistic factors contributing to
recognition disparities. Furthermore, we aim to explore fairness-aware data augmentation and
domain adaptation strategies to mitigate these biases. The outcome of this project is to develop
a comprehensive framework toward inclusive and equitable ASR technologies, ensuring robust
speech recognition performance across all socioeconomic groups.
